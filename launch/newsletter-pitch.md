# Newsletter Pitch — Cold Email to Sports/AI Newsletters

Send this 1 week before public launch. Replies are slow; the lead time
matters. The goal: 1–2 mentions in newsletters with 5K+ subscribers.
Each mention drives 100–500 referral visits and a few real backlinks
that compound for months.

## Targets to research first

Sports/betting newsletters:
- **Action Network** (their newsletter)
- **The Athletic** (sports + analytics angle)
- **Substack: Action Sports / The Lines / SBD Daily**
- **Sharp Football Analysis**
- **Frontoffice Sports** (industry/business angle, more press-friendly)

AI/tech newsletters with curious-about-AI audience:
- **Ben's Bites** (daily AI roundup, very link-happy)
- **The Rundown AI** (huge audience, looking for tools)
- **TLDR AI**
- **Superhuman AI**
- **Marginal Revolution** (Tyler Cowen — long shot but he's covered Kalshi)
- **Stratechery** (Ben Thompson — long shot, only if you have a strong angle)

Prediction-market specific:
- **Marginal Revolution** (Cowen + prediction markets)
- **Slow Boring** (Yglesias has covered Kalshi)
- **Astral Codex Ten** (Scott Alexander, very prediction-market-friendly)

## Subject lines (test 2-3)

> An AI prediction engine for sports — like Kalshi but for sports
> Built the AI prediction layer sports has been missing
> Show me one calibrated 75% sports pick that actually wins 75% of the time

## Personalization tips (do not skip)

- Reference a specific piece they wrote in the last 30 days. Not "I love your work" — name the piece.
- Adjust the angle for the publication: AI newsletters get the model angle, sports newsletters get the calibration/CLV angle, prediction-market newsletters get the Kalshi/Polymarket comparison.
- Don't attach files. Don't include UTM links. Don't add a video. Three things ruin pitch emails: attachments, marketing-speak, and asking for a follow-up before they've replied to the first one.

## Follow-up

If no reply in 7 days, send one (1) follow-up:

> Quick bump on the below — also want to make sure the email made it through. If timing's bad or it's not a fit, totally understand, just want to confirm. Otherwise — happy to send the most relevant materials based on your beat.

That's it. After two emails with no reply, move on. Don't send a third.

## Track who reads

If you're being formal about it:
- Use Hunter.io or Apollo to find direct emails (avoid info@ / press@)
- Use a free tool like MailTracker or HubSpot Free to know if they opened (don't ping them based on opens — just for your own data)
- Spreadsheet: Name, Publication, Email, Sent date, Opened?, Replied?, Notes
